# Launch Plan — タスクリー (Taskly)

---

## Pre-Launch Checklist (T-4 weeks to launch)

### T-4 weeks
- [ ] App Store Connect: complete all metadata fields (name, subtitle, description, keywords, screenshots)
- [ ] Prepare 6.5" and 5.5" iPhone screenshots (at least 3, up to 10) — show real UI, not mockup illustrations
- [ ] Prepare App Preview video (30 seconds max) — show a group being created, tasks assigned, and completed in one flow
- [ ] Set up TestFlight beta with 10–20 real users from each target segment (サークル, 趣味グループ, 店舗)
- [ ] Create social accounts: @taskly_jp on X and Instagram if not already live
- [ ] Draft 5 pre-launch X/Instagram posts (tease the problem, not the app)

### T-3 weeks
- [ ] Beta feedback round 1 complete — address critical UX issues
- [ ] Write note.com article draft: "5人のチームにSlackは大げさだと気づくまで" (hook: problem-first, tool reveal at end)
- [ ] Identify 10–15 university サークル accounts on X/Instagram to reach out to at launch
- [ ] Identify 5 small business owner accounts on X/Instagram to reach out to at launch
- [ ] Prepare Product Hunt submission assets (see Product Hunt section below)
- [ ] Set up Apple Search Ads account; load creatives and keyword lists from keywords.md

### T-2 weeks
- [ ] Submit app to App Store review (allow buffer for rejection/resubmit)
- [ ] Beta feedback round 2 — fix anything blocking first-run experience
- [ ] Finalize note.com article; identify 2–3 tech bloggers who cover productivity/indie apps to pitch
- [ ] Prepare "launch week" social content (5–7 posts, not just promotional — show use cases)
- [ ] Draft DM templates for outreach to clubs and shops
- [ ] Confirm App Store approval and release date

### T-1 week
- [ ] Schedule all launch-day social posts
- [ ] Brief any personal network contacts (developers, students, managers) to download on day 1
- [ ] Set up a way to collect feedback (Typeform, Google Form, or in-app link)
- [ ] Prepare a "launch achieved" post for X (real download numbers build trust in indie community)
- [ ] Final QA on the live App Store page — test the install flow yourself

## Launch Day Playbook

### X (Twitter)
**Post 1 — 09:00 JST:**
「タスクリー、リリースしました。グループでタスクとルーティンを共有するアプリです。LINEで流れていくタスクに疲れたチームに。無料で使えます。[App Store link] #タスクリー #アプリリリース」

**Post 2 — 12:00 JST (use case thread):**
3–4ツイートのスレッドで「どんなチームが使うか」を具体例で語る。広告感を出さず、「こんな課題持ってる人いませんか」というトーンで。

**Post 3 — 19:00 JST (evening engagement):**
「リリースから○時間経ちました。もしよかったら感想教えてください」← 実際の数字と温度感を出す。

- Feed投稿：アプリのUI画面 + キャプションでユースケース紹介（部活編）
- ストーリー：「NEW APP」スタンプ + 「タップしてAppStoreへ」リンクスタンプ
- リール：コンセプト#1 or #5の動画を完成させて公開（幹事のLINE地獄）

- コンセプト#2（スプレッドシート誰も開かない）を公開。コメントで共感を集める
- 説明欄にApp Storeリンクとプロフィールリンク案内

## PR Angles

### note.com 記事
**タイトル案:**
1. 「スタッフ5人のお店に、NotionもSlackも大げさすぎた話」
2. 「サークル幹事を3年やって気づいた、LINEがタスク管理に向いていない理由」

**構成:** 問題提起（共感）→ 試したツールとなぜ合わなかったか → タスクリーの思想説明 → 実際の使い方 → 「試してみてください」

- **ターゲット媒体:** Lifehacker Japan, iPhone Mania, AppBank, ASCII, はてなブックマーク経由のブログ
- **ピッチ角度:** 「グループ向けタスク管理の空白地帯を狙ったインディーアプリ」「Notion・Slackに疲れた人向けのシンプル代替」
- **提供できるもの:** 早期アクセス、開発者インタビュー（アプリ開発の経緯）、スクリーンショット素材一式

## Product Hunt Submission Checklist

- [ ] **Tagline (60 chars max):** "Group tasks and routines. Without the chat chaos." (JA: 「チームのタスクを、チャットから出そう」)
- [ ] **Description:** 3段落。問題→解決→誰向けか。300ワード以内
- [ ] **Gallery images (minimum 3):** ①グループホーム画面 ②タスク割り当て画面 ③ルーティン完了画面
- [ ] **Maker comment:** Personal story, why this was built, what type of feedback you're looking for
- [ ] **Launch day hunter:** Ask a well-followed Product Hunt user to hunt it, or self-hunt and be ready to respond to all comments within the first 2 hours
- [ ] **Timing:** Launch on Product Hunt at 00:01 PST (Product Hunt resets at midnight PT) — ideally Tuesday or Wednesday for best visibility
- [ ] **Prep supporters:** Ask 15–20 people in your network to upvote on launch day (not DM-blast — personal asks only)

## Week 1 Post-Launch Goals

### Download targets (realistic indie app, Japan market)
- Day 1: 50–100 downloads (personal network + launch day posts)
- Day 3: 150–200 total (organic + word of mouth starting)
- Day 7: 300–500 total (if even one SNS post gains traction)
- Week 1 stretch goal: 1,000 downloads (requires one piece of content to go semi-viral)

*Note: These are realistic cold-start targets, not funded-campaign targets. Each download from a genuine target user is worth 10 from random users.*

### Rating / Review Acquisition Strategy
- Do NOT pop an in-app review prompt on first open. Trigger it after:
  - User has created a group AND added at least 2 tasks (activation moment), OR
  - User has been in the app for 3+ days
- Directly ask beta users and early adopters to leave a review via personal message (not a blast)
- Respond to every review in the first month — especially criticism
- Target: 10 ratings by end of week 1. Even 4-star average is fine; zero ratings is a trust killer

### User Feedback Collection Plan
- Embed a "フィードバックを送る" link in app settings → Google Form or Typeform
- Week 1 survey questions (keep to 3 questions max):
  1. どんなグループで使いましたか？（サークル / 趣味グループ / 仕事チーム / その他）
  2. 使いはじめて、何が一番よかったですか？
  3. 使いにくかった点や、あったら嬉しい機能はありますか？
- DM 5–10 active users personally at the end of week 1 for a 10-minute voice/text call

### When to Spend on Apple Search Ads

| Phase | Timing | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Discovery | Launch day → Week 2 | Search Match campaign: JPY 500–1,000/day budget, collect keyword data |
| Optimization | Week 3–4 | Review search terms report; identify converting keywords; add to Exact Match |
| Scale | Month 2+ | Double budget on Tier 1 exact match terms that convert above 3% tap-to-install |
| Seasonal push | April (新歓), September (後期サークル), March (引き継ぎ) | Increase budget 2–3x for サークル targeting around these dates |

**Key rule:** Don't scale spend until tap-to-install rate is above 3% organically. Paid traffic amplifies what's already working — it doesn't fix a broken conversion funnel.

### A/B Test Ideas for Screenshots

Run these sequentially (change one variable at a time):

1. **First screenshot: Problem-first vs. UI-first**
   - A: テキストオーバーレイ「LINEでのタスク管理、もう終わりにしよう」+ アプリ画面
   - B: クリーンなアプリUI画面のみ、短いキャプション

2. **Color/framing of screens:**
   - A: ライトモード
   - B: ダークモード
   *(check which your target demographic uses more)*

3. **Use case focus in screenshots:**
   - A: サークル向けシナリオ（「展示会準備」などのラベル入り）
   - B: 汎用シナリオ（職種・用途を特定しないラベル）

4. **Screenshot count:** 3 vs. 6 — test whether more screens increases or drops conversion

## Metrics to Track

### App Store Conversion
| Metric | Target | How to measure |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Impression → Product Page view rate | >30% | App Store Connect |
| Product Page view → Download rate | >30% | App Store Connect |
| Overall impression → download | >8–10% | App Store Connect |

*If product page view rate is low → fix screenshots/title*
*If download rate from page view is low → fix description or social proof (ratings)*

### Retention
| Metric | Realistic target (Month 1) | Healthy target (Month 3+) |
|--------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| D1 retention | 40% | 50% |
| D7 retention | 20% | 30% |
| D30 retention | 10% | 18% |

*Retention benchmarks for utility/productivity apps. If D1 < 30%, investigate onboarding flow.*

### Activation Metric
- **Group creation rate:** % of installs that result in a group being created
  - Target: >50% within 24 hours of install
  - If lower: the "create a group" CTA in onboarding needs to be more prominent or the empty state needs a stronger nudge

- **Member invite rate:** % of group creators who invite at least 1 other person
  - Target: >60%
  - This is the most important virality signal — each invite is a potential new install

### Freemium-to-Paid Conversion
| Stage | Target rate | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Install → Free active user | 30% at D7 | "Active" = created a group + added tasks |
| Free active → hits limit | Track carefully | Hitting a limit is a conversion opportunity |
| Hits limit → Upgrades | >15% of users who hit limit | This rate measures whether your limit is well-calibrated |
| Overall install → paid | 2–4% (Month 3+) | Standard freemium benchmark for indie apps |

**Paywall trigger recommendation:** Show upgrade prompt when user tries to add task #11 or routine #6 (not before they've seen the value). Never interrupt mid-task — show after a completion moment.
