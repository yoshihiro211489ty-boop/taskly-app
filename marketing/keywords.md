## Match Type & Bid Strategy Notes

**Search Match (自動マッチ)**
- Use for initial discovery phase (first 2–4 weeks post-launch)
- Set a conservative daily budget ($5–10 USD equivalent) and let Apple find initial intent signals
- Review search term reports weekly to identify high-converting terms to add to Broad/Exact

**Broad Match**
- Use for Tier 2 keywords
- Good for reaching adjacent queries without manually listing every variation
- Examples: "タスク管理" will match "グループタスク管理アプリ 無料" etc.
- Set impression share limits to control spend

**Exact Match**
- Reserve for Tier 1, highest-intent keywords once validated
- Bids: Start at the suggested bid + 20% to win early auctions, then optimize down after 50+ taps
- Review weekly; pause anything with >100 taps and <2% conversion rate

**Campaign structure recommendation:**
```
Campaign 1: Japan - Tier 1 (Exact)      ← highest budget priority
Campaign 2: Japan - Tier 2 (Broad)      ← secondary budget
Campaign 3: Japan - Search Match        ← discovery + keyword mining
Campaign 4: US/EN - Tier 1 (Exact)     ← if pursuing English market
```

**Budget pacing:**
- Launch week: Heavier spend to gather data fast. Aim for 200+ taps in week 1
- Weeks 2–4: Analyze tap-to-download ratio. Cut anything below 2%
- Month 2+: Shift 70% budget to proven Tier 1 terms; keep 30% on Broad for new discovery

## ASO Keywords — Work Into Description Naturally

These are words and phrases that should appear organically in the App Store description (both Japanese and English) to support search ranking. Do not stuff them — weave them into actual sentences.

**Japanese phrases to include:**
- タスク管理 (task management)
- グループ共有 (group sharing)
- チームのルーティン (team routine)
- 進捗確認 (progress check)
- 役割分担 (role assignment)
- サークル (club/circle)
- 部活 (club activities)
- チェックリスト (checklist)
- 習慣 (habit)
- 担当者 (assignee/person in charge)

**English phrases to include:**
- group tasks
- shared routines
- team progress
- assign tasks
- checklist
- club organizer
- small team
- staff management
- daily routine
- shared to-do

**Placement priority:**
- First 2 sentences of description: highest weight (don't waste on brand fluff)
- Bullet points: second highest
- Use case paragraphs: natural density, no stuffing
- Title/Subtitle: highest weight of all — treat these as prime real estate
